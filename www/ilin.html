ilin {GSIF}R Documentation

NSCD soil samples for Illinois and Indiana States

Description

A subset of the National Cooperative Soil Characterization Database (NSCD) covering the Illinois and Indiana States (5189 soil profiles). Analytical procedures and methods of soil preparation are identified by methods codes in the column headings of the standard NSSC data sheets and are described in Soil Survey Laboratory Information Manual No. 42.

Usage

data(ilin)

Format

The ilin data set contains two data frames — sites and horizons. Sites table contains the following columns:

SOURCEID

factor; unique label to help a user identify a particular site (user_site_id in the NSCD)

LATNAD83

numeric; latitude in decimal degrees on the NAD83 datum

LONNAD83

numeric; longitude in decimal degrees on the NAD83 datum

TIMESTRR

Date; the date on which this particular soil was described or sampled (observation_date in the NSCD)

TAXSUSDA

factor; correlated name for soil series (correlated_taxon_name in the NSCD)

TAXKUSDA

factor; taxon type — soil series or taxadjunct (correlated_taxon_kind in the NSCD)

TAXNUSDA

factor; correlated Keys to Soil Taxonomy taxon complete name e.g. "Sandy, mixed, thermic Ustic Torriorthent" (correlated_class_name in the NSCD)

Horizons table contains the following columns:

SOURCEID

factor; a short label to help a user identify a particular site (user_site_id in the NSCD)

LSQINT

integer; a layer sequence number 1 to N (layer_sequence in the NSCD)

HZDUSD

character; pedological designation of the horizon e.g. "Bt1" (hzn_desgn in the NSCD)

UHDICM

numeric; lower horizon depth from the surface in cm (hzn_bot in the NSCD)

LHDICM

numeric; upper horizon depth from the surface in cm (hzn_top in the NSCD)

CLYPPT

numeric; weight percentage of the clay particles (<0.0002 mm; clay_tot_psa in the NSCD)

SNDPPT

numeric; weight percentage of the silt particles (0.0002–0.05 mm; sand_tot_psa in the NSCD)

SLTPPT

numeric; weight percentage of the sand particles (0.05–2 mm; silt_tot_psa in the NSCD)

CRFMAS

numeric; weight percentage of coarse fragments (> 2 mm; wpg2 in the NSCD)

PHIHO5

numeric; pH index measured in water solution(ph_h2o in the NSCD)

PHIKCL

numeric; pH index measured in KCl solution (ph_kcl in the NSCD)

ORCDRC

numeric; soil organic carbon content in permille (see below)

BLDODR

bulk density oven dry (105 C) in kg per cubic meter (db_od in the NSCD)

The ilin.grids data frame contains a list of covariates prepared for the Illinois and Indiana States (40,383 grid nodes):

CRPUSG2

numeric; percentage (0–1) of crop fields based on the National Land cover map from 2006

FORUSG2

numeric; percentage (0–1) of deciduous forest based on the National Land cover map from 2006

DINSRT2

numeric; diffuse insolation derived in SAGA GIS

INSSRT2

numeric; potential incoming solar radiation derived in SAGA GIS

LNDUSG2

numeric; land mask (0–1)

TRITOX2

numeric; kernel density of the Toxics Release Inventory (EPA) locations

TWISRT2

numeric; SAGA Topographic Wetness Index based on the SRTM DEM

SLPSRT2

numeric; slope in degrees based on the SRTM DEM

SORGSM2

factor; dominant suborder (Keys to Soil Taxonomy) class from the General Soil Map STATSGO2 (at scale 1:250,000) obtained from the NRCS data gateway

LON

numeric; x-coordinate (decimal degrees) in the WGS84 system

LAT

numeric; y-coordinate (decimal degrees) in the WGS84 system

Details

The NSCD data set is only a sample of the complete laboratory data. The original database contains over 30 thousand profiles. The variables listed above were selected for testing algorithms to produce soil property maps according to the GlobalSoilMap.net specifications. ORCDRC values are based on the c_tot and oc values in the NSCD. The total organic carbon in permille for alkaline soils (pH in water > 7) has been corrected by removing 12 percent of weight percentage of CaCO3. Note

Majority of the gridded layers listed were obtained from the USGS Seamless data warehouse. The percentage of crop fields and the land mask (0–100 percent) were derived using the area weighted averaging from the 30 m resolution National Land cover map from 2006. The land mask (0–100 percent) shows percentage of water bodies, medium and high intensity build-up (urban) areas. To create a boolean mask map, consider using some treshold value (e.g. 0.6). Density of the industrial facilities / pollutants (Toxics Release Inventory) were obtained from the U.S. National Library of Medicine. Land surface parameters diffuse insolation, potential incoming solar radiation, SAGA Topographic Wetness Index and slope map were derived in SAGA GIS using the 250 m resolution SRTM DEM obtained from the Consultative Group on International Agricultural Research (CGIAR), then resampled to 3/120 arcdegrees (2.5 km resolution). Soil suborder layer was derived as dominant taxonomic components (highest percentage) in the General Soil Map STATSGO2 (at scale 1:250,000). The processing steps used to prepare the map of dominatant taxonomic compenents are described in this script. There are cases where the dominant suborder is less than 30% of the map unit, which means that selecting a single suborer is not likely all that informative. To obtain additional covariate layers, e.g. SRTM DEM, MODIS, GlobCov and similar publicly available global data sets refer to the WorldGrids.org repository. Listed gridded layers follow a standard naming convention used by WorlGrids.org (the standard 8.3 filename convention with at most eight characters): first three letter are used for the variable type e.g. CRP (percentage (0–1) of crop fields); the next three letters represent the data source or collection method e.g. USG (USGS); the 6th character is the effective scale e.g. 2 indicates the 3rd standard scale i.e. 3/120 decimal degrees (about 2.5 km).

Examples

data(ilin)
coordinates(ilin$sites) <- ~ LONNAD83 + LATNAD83
proj4string(ilin$sites) <- CRS("+proj=longlat +datum=NAD83")
## Not run: # obtain state borders:
require(maps)
states.m = map('state', plot=FALSE, fill=TRUE)
IDs <- sapply(strsplit(states.m$names, ":"), function(x) x[1])
require(maptools)
states <- as(map2SpatialPolygons(states.m, IDs=IDs), "SpatialLines")
# overlay and plot points and maps:
plot(spTransform(ilin$sites, CRS("+proj=longlat +datum=WGS84")), pch="+", cex=.6, col="black")
lines(states)
# download the gridded data:
download.file("http://worldgrids.org/rda/ilin.grids.rda", "ilin.grids.rda")
load("ilin.grids.rda")
gridded(ilin.grids) <- ~LON+LAT
proj4string(ilin.grids) <- CRS("+proj=longlat +datum=WGS84")
# obtain state borders:
require(maps)
states.m = map('state', plot=FALSE, fill=TRUE)
IDs <- sapply(strsplit(states.m$names, ":"), function(x) x[1])
require(maptools)
states <- as(map2SpatialPolygons(states.m, IDs=IDs), "SpatialLines")
# plot some layers:
lst = c("CRPUSG2","FORUSG2")
col = grey(rev(seq(0.1,0.975,0.025)))
spplot(ilin.grids[lst], col.regions=col, sp.layout=list("sp.lines", states))
spplot(ilin.grids["SORGSM2"], sp.layout=list("sp.lines", states))
# write to a GIS format:
writeGDAL(ilin.grids["FORUSG2"], "FORUSG2.tif", "GTiff", mvFlag=-99999)

## End(Not run)
