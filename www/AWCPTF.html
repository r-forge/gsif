AWCPTF {GSIF}R Documentation

Available soil water capacity

Description

Derive available soil water capacity (in cubic-meter per cubic-meter) based on a Pedo-Transfer Function developed using the Africa Soil Profile Database (Wösten et al. 2013).

Usage

AWCPTF(SNDPPT, SLTPPT, CLYPPT, ORCDRC, 
    BLD=1682, CEC, PHIHOX, h1=-10, h2=-20, h3=-31.6, 
    pwp=-1585, PTF.coef, fix.values=TRUE )

Arguments

SNDPPT

numeric; sand content in percent

SLTPPT

numeric; silt content in percent

CLYPPT

numeric; clay content in percent

ORCDRC

numeric; soil organic carbon concentration in permille or g / kg

BLD

numeric; bulk density in kg / cubic-meter for the horizon/solum

CEC

numeric; Cation Exchange Capacity in cmol per kilogram

PHIHOX

numeric; soil pH in water suspension

h1

numeric; moisture potential in kPa e.g. -10 (pF 2.0)

h2

numeric; moisture potential in kPa e.g. -20 (pF 2.3)

h3

numeric; moisture potential in kPa e.g. -31.6 (pF 2.5)

pwp

numeric; moisture potential at wilting point in kPa e.g. -1585 (pF 4.2)

PTF.coef

data.frame; optional conversion coefficients (Pedo-Transfer Function)

fix.values

logical; specifies whether to correct values of textures and bulk density to avoid creating non-sensical values

Value

Returns a data frame with the following columns:

  • AWCh1: available soil water capacity (volumetric fraction) for h1;

  • AWCh2: available soil water capacity (volumetric fraction) for h2;

  • AWCh3: available soil water capacity (volumetric fraction) for h3;

  • WWP: available soil water capacity (volumetric fraction) until wilting point;

Note

Pedotransfer coefficients (PTF.coef) developed by Hodnett and Tomasella (2002). fix.values will correct sand, silt and clay fractions so they sum up to 100, and will replace bulk density values using global minimum maximum values.

Author(s)

Johan Leenaars and Maria Ruiperez Gonzalez

References

  • Hodnett, M. G., & Tomasella, J. (2002). Marked differences between van Genuchten soil water-retention parameters for temperate and tropical soils: a new water-retention pedo-transfer functions developed for tropical soils. Geoderma, 108(3), 155-180.

  • Wösten, J. H. M., Verzandvoort, S. J. E., Leenaars, J. G. B., Hoogland, T., & Wesseling, J. G. (2013). Soil hydraulic information for river basin studies in semi-arid regions. Geoderma, 195, 79-86.

Examples

SNDPPT = 30 
SLTPPT = 25 
CLYPPT = 48 
ORCDRC = 23 
BLD = 1200 
CEC = 12 
PHIOX = 6.4
x <- AWCPTF(SNDPPT, SLTPPT, CLYPPT, ORCDRC, BLD, CEC, PHIHOX)
x
## predict AWC for the whole AfSP DB:
data(afsp)
names(afsp$horizons) 
BLDf <- ifelse(is.na(afsp$horizons$BLD), 
   mean(afsp$horizons$BLD, na.rm=TRUE), afsp$horizons$BLD)
xd <- AWCPTF(afsp$horizons$SNDPPT, afsp$horizons$SLTPPT, 
  afsp$horizons$CLYPPT, afsp$horizons$ORCDRC, 
  BLD=BLDf*1000, afsp$horizons$CEC, afsp$horizons$PHIHOX)
str(xd)
